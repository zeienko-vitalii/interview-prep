## How to use this document

- Treat this as cue cards, not a script to memorize word-for-word.
- In most answers, use this pattern:
  1. answer directly
  2. give one short real example
  3. tie it back to how you would help on their app
- Keep your pace calm. Short answers are better than trying to prove everything at once.
- If they ask something outside your exact ownership, keep the boundary clear instead of stretching the truth.
- Good anchors for this call:
  - existing Flutter codebases
  - iOS and Android delivery
  - backend and third-party integrations
  - maintainable structure
  - documentation
  - ambiguity handling
  - English and Russian communication
- If the conversation briefly switches to Russian, use the fallback lines below and then continue naturally.

  - you can reason beyond widgets
  - you can handle real mobile product complexity

## Likely mixed technical questions

### How do you approach an existing Flutter codebase in the first week?

- Interviewer intent:
  - check whether you can onboard safely and practically
- Good answer shape:
  - understand build and environments
  - identify critical flows and current pain points
  - avoid premature refactoring
- Cue-card answer:
  - `In the first week, I’d try to understand how the app builds, how environments are managed, what the critical user flows are, and where the current pain points are. I’d look at crash patterns, unstable areas, and integration boundaries before making structural changes. The goal early on is clarity and safe prioritization, not rewriting.`

### How do you decide whether to refactor or keep the current structure?

- Interviewer intent:
  - test pragmatism
- Good answer shape:
  - preserve delivery
  - refactor where risk or maintenance cost is real
- Cue-card answer:
  - `My default is to keep the current structure unless it is clearly causing delivery problems, instability, or repeated confusion. I prefer incremental refactoring around high-friction areas rather than broad restructuring, especially in an active product.`

### How do you handle API integrations and third-party services?

- Interviewer intent:
  - assess integration maturity
- Good answer shape:
  - clear boundaries
  - predictable error handling
  - testability
- Cue-card answer:
  - `I try to keep service integration separate from UI and state handling, so the code stays easier to reason about and test. For me the important part is clear boundaries, predictable error handling, and making sure failures in one integration don’t leak through the whole app.`

### What is your experience with payments, chat, and notifications?

- Interviewer intent:
  - see how close your experience is to their needs
- Good answer shape:
  - be accurate about scope
  - mention adjacent experience honestly
- Cue-card answer:
  - `I’ve worked on payment-related flows and on in-app messaging functionality, and I’m comfortable with the mobile-side structure needed for those kinds of features. I haven’t framed my work as owning a full marketplace stack end to end, but the integration and maintainability side is familiar to me.`

### How do you keep Flutter code maintainable?

- Interviewer intent:
  - check whether you think beyond feature delivery
- Good answer shape:
  - boundaries
  - readability
  - documentation
  - testing where it matters
- Cue-card answer:
  - `For me, maintainability starts with clear boundaries between presentation, state, and service layers. I also care about readable code, practical documentation for flows that are easy to misunderstand, and enough testing around risky areas so changes stay safe over time.`

### How do you debug crashes and unstable flows?

- Interviewer intent:
  - check real-world debugging approach
- Good answer shape:
  - reproduce
  - narrow scope
  - verify fix
- Cue-card answer:
  - `I usually start by reproducing the issue as clearly as possible, then I narrow it down by checking the flow boundaries, async behaviour, navigation, and service calls involved. Once I have a likely cause, I want the fix to be small, verifiable, and covered enough that the same issue does not keep reappearing.`

### How do you think about performance on Android devices?

- Interviewer intent:
  - assess practical Flutter performance awareness
- Good answer shape:
  - profile before guessing
  - focus on jank sources
  - explain tradeoffs
- Cue-card answer:
  - `I try not to guess about performance too early. I’d first look at where the real cost is coming from, for example heavy rebuilds, list rendering, image work, repeated requests, or too much work on the UI thread. Then I’d make the smallest changes that improve responsiveness without making the codebase harder to maintain.`

### What is your AWS experience and where do you draw the boundary?

  - check honesty and collaboration around cloud ownership
- Good answer shape:
  - strongest area is mobile
  - comfortable in existing cloud-backed setup
  - coordinate with backend or DevOps

  - `My strongest area is the mobile side, especially Flutter product work, integrations, and release-safe delivery. I’m comfortable working inside an existing cloud-backed system and handling the mobile responsibilities around configuration, API contracts, release flow, and monitoring, while coordinating with backend or DevOps for infrastructure-side changes.`

### How do you test and ship fixes safely?

- Interviewer intent:
  - check whether you can stabilize the product rather than just patch it
- Good answer shape:
  - match test depth to risk
  - smoke-test critical paths
  - think about rollback and monitoring
- Cue-card answer:
  - `I usually match the testing depth to the risk of the change. For critical fixes, I want coverage around the risky logic, confidence on the affected screens or flows, and a clear release checklist for the main user paths. I also want monitoring and a rollback mindset, especially if the issue touches navigation, search, checkout, or messaging.`

## Questions to ask the client

Ask 4 to 6 of these, not all of them.

- `What are the biggest pain points in the app today: feature delivery, crashes, performance, or release process?`
- `How is the current Flutter codebase structured, and are there any parts you already consider risky or difficult to change?`
- `What backend stack and AWS services are already in place, and what level of cloud ownership do you expect from the mobile role?`
- `Which third-party services are live today: payments, chat, notifications, analytics, or something else?`
- `What would you want the person joining this role to improve first in the first few weeks?`
- `How does the team usually work across English and Russian in day-to-day communication?`
