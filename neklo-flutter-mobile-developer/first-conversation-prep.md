# First Conversation Prep

## Your Goal

Keep it light and clear:
- confirm fit
- sound senior and easy to work with
- avoid overexplaining
- make them want to move you forward

## 30-Second Intro

Hi, thanks for taking the time. I’m a Senior Flutter Developer with 8 years of
commercial mobile development experience. My recent work has mostly been around
existing production apps, cross-platform delivery, architecture, native
integrations when Flutter alone isn’t enough, and collaborating closely with
product, QA, and backend teams. A lot of my experience is in high-ambiguity
environments where the goal is to turn unclear requirements into stable,
maintainable mobile solutions.

## Why You're Interested

What caught my attention is that this isn’t just a standard Flutter UI role. It
combines Flutter with native mobile concerns, migration work, BLE and
location-related functionality, and real-world use cases. That fits well with
my background, especially where Flutter has to work together with device
capabilities and production constraints.

## What To Emphasize

Keep repeating these themes naturally:
- existing codebases, not only greenfield
- Flutter + native boundary work
- architecture and maintainability
- backend/API collaboration
- ambiguity handling
- release readiness

## Best Experience Summary For This Role

I’m strongest when I join an existing or evolving product, understand the
current technical and product constraints, and then help move it forward in a
clean and reliable way. I’ve worked on Flutter applications that required
architecture discipline, platform integrations, documentation, and coordination
with multiple stakeholders. I also have a native Android background, which
helps when mobile behavior goes beyond what Flutter abstracts well.

## Likely Questions And Good Answer Shape

### Tell me about your background

Keep it short:
- 8 years mobile
- recent years focused on Flutter
- Android native background
- production apps, architecture, integrations, collaboration

### Why are you interested in this role?

Say:
- hybrid Flutter/native nature is relevant
- location/sensor/device workflows are interesting
- not just UI, but real mobile engineering

### Have you worked with BLE / GPS / sensors?

Be honest and frame adjacency well:
- yes on native integrations and device-related work
- strongest concrete example: Sensibo
- comfortable working at the platform boundary when Flutter packages are not
  enough

### Do you have migration experience?

Say:
- yes, I’ve worked on products where legacy behavior had to be translated
  carefully into Flutter
- I’m comfortable working inside an established architecture and preserving
  product behavior while improving implementation quality

### Are you stronger in Flutter or native?

Say:
- Flutter is my main stack
- Android native is a strong supporting skill
- I’m comfortable making pragmatic decisions when native implementation is the
  better path

### Do you have iOS native experience?

Be careful here:
- don’t oversell
- say you have experience with native integrations and iOS-side collaboration,
  but keep Flutter as your main area if that’s the truth

A safe version:

My main focus is Flutter, with stronger native depth on Android. On iOS, I’m
comfortable working with integrations and platform-specific behavior when
needed, but I wouldn’t position myself as primarily a native iOS engineer.

### Are you comfortable with ambiguity?

This is one of your best areas. Say:

Yes. In a few recent projects, part of the work was clarifying product behavior
when requirements were incomplete or legacy logic was unclear. My usual
approach is to reduce ambiguity quickly, document decisions, and then implement
in a way that is stable and maintainable.

## Questions You Should Ask

Ask 4 to 6, not too many.

Good ones:
- What is the current state of the mobile product today?
- Is the Flutter app already in progress, or is this earlier in the migration?
- How much of the current logic still lives in native apps?
- Which areas are the highest priority right now: migration, new features,
  performance, or device integrations?
- How large is the mobile/backend team?
- How deep is the RTLS / BLE / telemetry work expected to be for this role?
- What would success look like in the first few months?

## What To Avoid

- Don’t claim deep RTLS expertise if it’s only adjacent
- Don’t overclaim Swift/iOS depth
- Don’t make it sound like you only want pure Flutter UI work
- Don’t give long project monologues unless asked

## Good Closing

At the end, say something like:

The role sounds genuinely interesting to me because it sits in that space
between Flutter product development and deeper mobile engineering. From what
you described, I think my background is quite relevant, especially around
cross-platform delivery, native integrations, and working through ambiguous
product requirements.
