# Smart Pauses During Meeting

Generated on: April 2, 2026
Purpose: quick guidance for how to pause, think, react naturally, and avoid
overreacting during the call.

## Main Rule

You do not need to answer instantly.

Fast answers are not always better answers.

A calm person usually:
- listens fully
- pauses briefly
- answers in a structured way

That sounds much better than reacting too fast.

## What To Say During A Pause

These are good natural phrases:

- `Let me think for a second.`
- `That’s a good question.`
- `The way I’d frame that is...`
- `I’d split that into two parts.`
- `From my side, the most relevant part is...`
- `A practical example there would be...`

These buy you time without sounding weak.

## If You Need More Time

Also normal:

- `I’d like a second to think about that properly.`
- `Let me think about the best example from my experience.`
- `I want to answer that carefully.`

These sound:
- thoughtful
- deliberate
- mature

Not:
- panicked
- lost
- underprepared

## How Not To Overreact

Do not do this:

- answer immediately just to fill silence
- laugh nervously after every question
- say `oh wow`, `hmm`, `uh`, `I don’t know`, or `sorry`
- start with a long disclaimer
- keep talking when you already made the point

A better pattern is:

1. small pause
2. short framing sentence
3. answer
4. stop

Example:

`That’s a good question. Let me think for a second.`
`I think the most relevant example from my side is Sensibo, because there the
challenge was...`

## Good Reactions When They Explain Something

You do not need to react a lot.

You only need to sound engaged and stable.

Good light reactions:

- `That makes sense.`
- `Understood.`
- `That’s helpful context.`
- `I see.`
- `Right, that makes sense.`
- `That’s useful to know.`

That is enough.

## How Not To Overdo Friendliness

You do not need:

- too many `great`, `amazing`, `awesome`
- repeated `thank you so much`
- exaggerated excitement

Too much enthusiasm can sound nervous.

Better:
- warm
- attentive
- measured

## If You Do Not Know Something Exactly

Do not rush.

Do not fake.

Say:

- `I wouldn’t want to overclaim direct experience there, but the adjacent part I do know well is...`
- `That’s not the strongest headline area in my background, but from the mobile side I’d approach it by...`
- `I haven’t worked with that exact setup, but a comparable case from my experience is...`

This is one of the best ways to sound trustworthy.

## If The Question Is Broad

You can control it by structuring it:

- `I’d break that into two parts.`
- `There’s the technical side, and there’s the product side.`
- `From the mobile perspective, I’d think about it this way...`

That helps you avoid rambling.

- stop

That alone will make you sound much more controlled.
